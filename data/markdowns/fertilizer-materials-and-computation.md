# FERTILIZER TERMINOLOGIES

*   **Fertilizer** – any substance which is added to the soil or applied to the plant to supply the elements essential for plant nutrition

*   **Fertilizer grade** – the guaranteed percentage of total nitrogen (N), available phosphorus (P<sub>2</sub>O<sub>5</sub>), and water soluble potassium (K<sub>2</sub>O), in that order, in a fertilizer material

# FERTILIZER TERMINOLOGIES

*   **Total plant food** – the total percentage of nutrients (usually of N,P,K and S) in a fertilizer material

17-0-17-12 = 46% total plant food
14-14-14-12 = 54% total plant food

*   **Carrier** – a material that is necessary to hold the fertilizer element in a form suitable for use

100% – 46% total plant food = 54% carrier

![PhilRice logo](image)

# FERTILIZER TERMINOLOGIES

*   **Filler** – a material used to provide the fertilizer with the desired amount of nutrient per unit of weight, to make application easier, or to reduce the absorption of water from the air

*   **Fertilizer recommendation** – the amount (kg) of N, P<sub>2</sub>O<sub>5</sub>, and K<sub>2</sub>O recommended per hectare

90+30+60 = 90 kg N, 30 kg P<sub>2</sub>O<sub>5</sub>, and 60 kg K<sub>2</sub>O per hectare application

# According to form

2. **Chemical or inorganic** – any fertilizer material which is natural or synthetic product of chemical reaction of certain raw materials.

* Natural chemical – rock phosphate, Chilean nitrate of soda, most potassium materials

* Synthetic chemical – ammonium sulfate, ordinary superphosphate, ammonium phosphate, complete fertilizer; Urea is synthetic but in organic form.

![PHILRICE logo](image)

# According to number of fertilizer elements present

1. <span style="color: #7FB3D5">Single fertilizers</span> – contain only one of the 3 major fertilizer elements (N,P, and K)

*Examples: Urea, Ammonium sulfate, ordinary superphosphate, muriate of potash, sulfate of potash*

2. <span style="color: #7FB3D5">Incomplete fertilizers</span> – contain 2 of the 3 major fertilizer elements

*Examples: ammonium phosphate, 17-0-17, potassium nitrate, potassium phosphate, urea superphosphate (20-10-0)*

![PHILRICE logo](image)

# According to number of fertilizer elements present

3. <span style="color: #76B5C5">Complete fertilizers</span> – contain all the 3 major fertilizer elements

*Examples: 14-14-14; 17-7-17; 10-15-15; 12-12-12*

4. <span style="color: #76B5C5">Mixed fertilizers</span> – contain 2 or more of the major fertilizer elements that are supplied by 2 or more fertilizer materials

*Examples: ammonium phosphate + muriate of potash; urea superphosphate (20-10-0)*

# **Option 1.** To calculate the amount of fertilizer materials for 1 ha at the recommended rate of 90 + 30 + 60 using a

<span style="color: #729fcf">combination of single fertilizers</span>:

![A cartoon rice grain character looking confused with a question mark above its head.](page_15_image_1_v2.jpg)

* 21-0-0 (ammonium sulfate)

* 0-18-0 (ordinary superphosphate)

* 0-0-60 (muriate of potash or potassium chloride)

![PHILRICE logo](image)

$$ \text{Amount of 21-0-0 (kg)} = \frac{90 \text{ kg N/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.21} = 428.6 $$

$$ \text{Amount of 0-18-0 (kg)} = \frac{30 \text{ kg P}_2\text{O}_5\text{/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.18} = 166.7 $$

$$ \text{Amount of 0-0-60 (kg)} = \frac{60 \text{ kg K}_2\text{O/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.60} = 100.0 $$

![A winking yellow smiley face giving two thumbs up.](page_16_image_2_v2.jpg)

Therefore, Mang Ado must apply 428.6 kg 21-0-0, 166.7 kg 0-18-0, and 100.0 kg 0-0-60 to satisfy the recommended rate of 90 + 30 + 60.

![PHILRICE logo](page_16_image_3_v2.jpg)

**Option 2.** To calculate the amount of fertilizer materials for 1 ha at the recommended rate of 90 + 30 + 60 using a <span style="color: #729fcf">combination of incomplete and single fertilizers</span>:

* 16-20-0 (ammonium phosphate

* 46-0-0 (urea)

* 0-0-60 (muriate of potash)

![A cartoon rice grain character wearing a jacket, jeans, and boots, looking sad while writing in a notebook.](page_17_image_1_v2.jpg)

![PHILRICE logo](image)

$$ \text{Amount of 16-20-0 (kg)} = \frac{30 \text{ kg P}_2\text{O}_5\text{/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.20} = 150.0 $$

$$ \text{Amount of 46-0-0 (kg)} = \frac{90 \text{ kg N} - \text{kg N from 150 kg 16-20-0/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.46} $$

$$ = \frac{90 - (150 \times 0.16)}{0.46} = \frac{90 - 24}{0.46} = \frac{66}{0.46} $$

$$ = 143.5 $$

$$ \text{Amount of 0-0-60 (kg)} = \frac{60 \text{ kg K}_2\text{O/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.60} = 100.0 $$

Therefore, he must apply 150.0 kg 16-20-0, 143.5 kg 46-0-0, and 100.0 kg 0-0-60 to satisfy the recommended rate of 90 + 30 + 60

**Option 3.** To calculate the amount of fertilizer materials for 1 ha at the recommended rate of 90 + 30 + 60 using a combination of complete and single fertilizers:

* 14-14-14 (complete)
* 46-0-0 (urea)
* 0-0-60 (muriate of potash)

![A cartoon character shaped like a grain of rice standing next to a stack of fertilizer bags.](page_19_image_2_v2.jpg)

![PHILRICE logo](image)

$$ \text{Amount of 14-14-14 (kg)} = \frac{30 \text{ kg P}_2\text{O}_5\text{/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.14} = \mathbf{214.3} $$

$$ \text{Amount of 46-0-0 (kg)} = \frac{90 \text{ kg N} - \text{kg N from 214.3 kg 14-14-14/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.46} $$

$$ = \frac{90 - (214.3 \times 0.14)}{0.46} = \frac{90 - 30}{0.46} = \frac{60}{0.46} = \mathbf{130.4} $$

$$ \text{Amount of 0-0-60 (kg)} = \frac{60 \text{ kg K}_2\text{O} - \text{kg K}_2\text{O from 214.3 kg 14-14-14/ha} \times 1 \text{ ha}}{0.60} $$

$$ = \frac{60 - (214.3 \times 0.14)}{0.60} = \frac{60 - 30}{0.60} = \frac{30}{0.60} = \mathbf{50.0} $$

Therefore, he must apply 214.3 kg 14-14-14, 130.4 kg 46-0-0, and 50.0 kg 0-0-60 to satisfy the recommended rate of 90 + 30 + 60
